Most students I know think about personal branding as something you worry about after graduation. I disagree. Building this website is part of that disagreement.

## The real purpose of a portfolio

A portfolio is not just a list of projects. It is a statement.

It says: I take my work seriously. I care about how I present myself. I understand that perception matters before someone has even spoken to me.

Recruiters, clients, and collaborators form an opinion before they read a single line of your resume. What they find when they search your name matters. And in Nepal's growing tech ecosystem, having an online presence is still unusual enough to be genuinely differentiating.

## Why I started early

I wanted to own my online presence before I needed it.

If someone searches "Sandesh Jamkatel," I want them to find a thoughtful developer with real work — not nothing, or worse, something from three years ago that no longer represents who I am.

Starting early also means I get to iterate. This site will be better in six months than it is today. If I had waited until after graduation to start, I would be building from zero under pressure, with no history and no examples of my thinking.

## What personal branding actually means

It does not mean self-promotion. It does not mean posting on LinkedIn every day or performing confidence you do not feel.

It means **clarity**. The ability to say, honestly and specifically:

- This is who I am
- This is what I build
- This is how I think about problems
- This is why you can trust my work

The best personal brands feel authentic because they are authentic. They do not overclaim. They show real work, real process, and a genuine point of view.

## The practical impact

Since building this site and shipping ShrinkBox, a few things have changed:

- I have better conversations with other developers online because I have something real to point to
- I think more carefully about the quality of my work, knowing it will be public
- I take my learning more seriously, because I intend to write about it
- I feel more confident reaching out to people, because I can show them something rather than just telling them

These are not small things. They compound.

## What I would say to other students

Start now. Your first version does not have to be perfect — it just has to be yours.

Put something real online. Write one honest blog post. Show one actual project, even if it is small. The credibility compounds over time. The student who starts at year one of BCA arrives at internship season with two years of public work history. That is hard to ignore.

The developers who get interesting opportunities are not always the most technically skilled. They are often the ones who bothered to show up publicly, consistently, and honestly.
